Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio

His dramatic use of chiaroscuro and intense realism depicted in his paintings started the Baroque Era

Caravaggio's artwork "Supper at Emmaus" painted in 1601 depicts the resurrected Jesus revealing himself to two of his disciples.

Below is a timeline of Caravaggio's life:

  • 1571 - Born in Milan, Italy.
  • 1576 - Family moved to Caravaggio 25 miles east of Milan to avoid a plague.
  • 1584 - Began his four year apprenticeship to painter Simone Peterzano.
  • 1592 - Left Caravaggio/Milan for Rome where there was a demand for paintings to fill the new churches and palazzos being built.
  • 1594 - Painting of "The Cardsharps" this painting was a milestone for Caravaggio in which this was one of his first paintings when he was attempting an independent career.
  • 1594-1595 - Painting of "The Fortune Teller" was one of his first compositions with more than one figure.
  • 1598 - Painting of "Martha and Mary Magdalene" was painted when Caravaggio was in the entourage of Cardinal Francesco Maria Del Monte.
  • 1599-1600 - Both paintings of "Calling of Saint Matthew" and "Martyrdom of Saint Matthew" were contracted to decorate the Contarelli Chapel. They were an immediate sensation and thereafter he never lacked commissions or patrons.
  • 1602 - Painting of "The Taking of Christ" originally commissioned by Roman nobleman Ciriaco Mattei is now housed in The National Gallery of Ireland, Dublin.
  • 1606 - Fled to Naples and exiled for killing a man named Tomassoni during a brawl.
  • 1607 - Painting of "Madonna of the Rosary" is the only painting by Caravaggio that could be called a standard Baroque altarpiece. The commissioner is uncertain possibly for a Dominican church. It is now housed in the Kunsthistorisches Museum in Vienna.
  • 1607 - Moved to Malta to secure a pardon for Tomassoni's death with the Knights of Saint John.
  • 1608 - Painting of "The Beheading of St John the Baptist" was his largest work and the only painting to which he put his signature. This painting is widely considered Caravaggio's masterpiece.
  • 1610 - Dies at the age of 38 under mysterious circumstances on his way from Naples to Rome after securing a pardon with the Pope.
